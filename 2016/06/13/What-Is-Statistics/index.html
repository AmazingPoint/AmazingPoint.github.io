What Is Meant by Statistics?

Definition

STATISTICS The science of collecting, organizing, presenting, analyzing, and interpreting data to assist in making more effective decisions.

Instance

For instance, Managers must make decisions about the quality of their product or service. For example:

customers call software companies for technical advice when they are not able to resolve an issue regarding the software. One measure of the quality of customer service is the time a customer must wait for a technical consultant to answer the call.
The company would then collect and analyze data on the response time. Does the typical response time differ by day of the week or time of day? If the response times are increasing, managers might decide to increase the number of technical consultants at particular times of the day or week.

Types of Statistics

The study statistics is usually devided into two categories: descriptive statistics and inferential statistics.

Descriptive Statistics

Definition

DESCRIPTIVE STATISTICS Method of orgnaizing, summarizing, and presenting data in an informative way.

Instance

For instance, The United States government reports the population of United States :

  • 1960 : 179,323,000
  • 1970 : 203,302,000
  • 1980 : 226,542,000
  • 1990 : 248,709,000
  • 2000 : 265,000,000
  • 2010 : 308,400,000

This information is decriptive statistics.
It is descriptive statistics if we calculate the percentage growth from one decade to next.However. It would not be descriptive statistics if we use these to estimate the population of the United States in the year 2020 or the percentage growth from 2010 to 2020.Why? The reson is these statistics are not being used to summarize past populations but to estimate future populations.

Inferential Statistics

INFERENTIAL STATISTICS The methods used to estimate a property of population on the basis of a sample.

A population may consist of individuals —such as all the CEOs from the Fortune 500 companies.

POPULATION The entire set of individuals or objects of interest or the measurements obtained from all individuals or objects of interest.

SAMPLE A portion, or part, of the population of interest.

The relationship between a sample and a population is portrayed below. For example, we wish to estimate the mean miles per gallon of SUVs. Six SUVs are selected from the population. The mean MPG of the six is used to estimate MPG for the population.
